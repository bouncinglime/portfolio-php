Lockr


First project for General Assembly's UXDi program.
This was the first project for the program. The goal was to interview a classmate to discover a problem that could be solved with a mobile app. My project partner had been wanting a simple place to store passwords that would reveal them without revealing the user name or the service at the same time. While there are a ton of password managers available, they are overkill for his needs. The basic idea was to be a glorified notepad that would also itself be locked.

Create a password-protected password manager app that allows easy reminder of username and password for various digital and physical services.

Clickable paper prototype of sketches for the app, along with basic error handling.

User interviews, research, and testing. Pen-and-paper sketches, both low and high fidelity.

POP App, online and on phone.


I was lucky that my partner had a rather robust idea of what app he wanted, and basically just had to design it. However, I was able to bring my QA experience to the fore and created some error handling that otherwise might have been missed. There was also a lot of back-and-forth conversation about the look and feel of the paper prototype before the final sketches were photographed for PopApp.
The original idea had been simply a password-protected place to record usernames and passwords. This is insecure if someone is looking over your shoulder, so we decided to have a hold-to-reveal action for passwords instead. Allowances were also made for entering codes (locks, gates, etc) that have no associated username.
Interviews revealed other information that would be useful to have in such a locker, such as answers to security questions. However, the complex interactions required for not revealing usernames at the same time as any of the security information made this too difficult.
